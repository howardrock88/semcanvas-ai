# Social Preview Design Philosophy: Clean Semantic Compare

Clean Semantic Compare treats the launch image as a product demonstration, not a poster full of claims. The layout is split into a restrained identity block and one large proof card, so viewers understand the promise and see the before/after result without decoding overlapping fragments.

The right side carries the evidence. The complete before/after comparison is presented as a single large card with generous margins, making the selected mask, generated result, and short instruction readable in GitHub, WeChat, and mobile feed previews.

The color system stays calm and precise: blue signals AI transformation, green appears only in the actual selected-mask example, and warm off-white keeps the product approachable. Decorative semantic markers were removed to avoid confusing viewers with nonfunctional UI elements.

Text is deliberately minimal: the name, the promise, one short edit prompt, and a concise quick-start cue. Everything else is carried by the large visual comparison.
