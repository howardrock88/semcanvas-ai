# Social Preview Design Philosophy: Semantic Glass

Semantic Glass treats image editing as a layer of intention floating above a finished visual world. The composition should feel local-first, tactile, and technically precise, with translucent surfaces that imply masks, selections, and inference without explaining them. Space is generous and deliberate: one side speaks through bold product typography, the other side through layered before/after image cards that reveal the workflow at a glance.

Color behaves like semantic annotation. Blue marks model intelligence, green marks user selection, and warm cream keeps the tool approachable rather than sterile. The palette is calibrated for trust and clarity, with painstaking attention to contrast, image legibility, and share-card readability on mobile feeds.

The visual rhythm is modular and engineered. Rounded panels, command pills, subtle grid points, and orbital mask markers create an artifact that feels meticulously crafted, as if every curve and spacing decision was adjusted by someone with deep product-design expertise. Text remains sparse and essential; the workflow is shown through spatial relationships rather than paragraphs.

The final work should feel like a premium local AI tool rather than a generic generative-art banner. It must appear carefully labored over: crisp edges, balanced hierarchy, disciplined typography, and enough visual intrigue that a viewer understands the product before reading the README.
